# Manager Conversation — Script, Pre-Read, Follow-Up

> Three artifacts for the conversation with [Manager Name] at MDLBEAST.
> Adjust voice to your relationship and the manager's communication style.

---

## How to use this

Run in this order, with these gaps:

  1. **Send the pre-read (Section 1) as a Slack DM 24-48 hours before the meeting.** Not the day before — gives them time to react privately rather than in the room. Not a week before — they will forget or overthink it.
  2. **Have the live conversation (Section 2) in your next scheduled 1:1.** Do not request a special meeting; that signals more weight than the conversation warrants. Use your existing slot.
  3. **Send the written follow-up (Section 3) within 4 hours of the meeting.** Same day. The written record is the deliverable you need; the conversation is the input.

Total time investment: 5 minutes drafting the pre-read, 15-20 minutes of conversation, 10 minutes sending follow-up. The thing you are buying with this is a clean break-glass option if anyone ever asks "did you tell your manager."

---

## Section 1 — Pre-read Slack DM

Sent 24-48 hours before the 1:1. Adjust tone if your relationship is more or less formal than peer.

> Hey [Manager Name], heads up before our next 1:1 — want to bring something up.
>
> For the past few weeks I have been exploring a side project: a B2B AI customer support tool for GCC live event operators (festivals, club nights, conferences). It is a layer that sits on top of any ticketing platform and handles event-window WhatsApp support, refund pressure, payment recovery.
>
> Where it is: validation phase only. No company, no team, no real customers, no branding. Built on synthetic demo data I invented — zero MDLBEAST data, code, customer info, or work hours involved.
>
> Why I am raising it: NOFOMO is in the same space and I want to be upfront with you. The product is not NOFOMO competition — it sits above ticketing, and NOFOMO would be one of the operators I would want as a customer eventually. But I would rather have this conversation with you early than have it come up sideways later.
>
> No agenda beyond alignment. Happy to walk through the demo if useful, or just talk through it. See you [day].

## Section 2 — Live conversation script

Total: 8-12 minutes of speaking time, then 5-10 minutes of listening.

### Opening (1 minute)

> Thanks for making time. As I mentioned in the DM, I want to be transparent about a side project I have been working on. Not asking for permission, not asking for anything specific today. Just want it in the open between us.

### What it is (3 minutes)

> The product is an AI agent for live event customer support in the GCC. WhatsApp-first, Arabic and English native. It handles the kind of questions that hit support teams during event week — what time do gates open, where is parking, my ticket did not arrive, I want a refund, my payment failed. The wedge is refund deflection and revenue recovery, not support efficiency. The pitch to a CFO is "we save you SAR X in refunds and recover SAR Y in failed payments per event," not "we automate your support."
>
> The market is GCC event operators outside MDLBEAST — Riyadh Season vendors, UAE event production firms, club operators, festival organizers. Total addressable I am tracking is around 30 operators in the region.
>
> Right now it is a working demo on a simulator. No real WhatsApp number, no real customers, no real operator running it. I am in a validation phase — talking to operators, refining the wedge — before any real money goes in.

### How I am running it (2 minutes)

> Three things I want to be explicit about, because they would be my concerns if I were you.
>
> First, time. This is weekends and evenings. Day job comes first, always has. The whole reason the validation phase looks the way it does is to keep the time commitment low until there is something real.
>
> Second, IP and data. I have been strict: zero MDLBEAST data, zero MDLBEAST code, zero customer information, zero internal documents in any artifact related to this. The demo content is all synthetic — generic festival and club events I invented. I have been deliberate about this from day one.
>
> Third, conflict. NOFOMO is a ticketing platform. What I am building is a support layer that sits on top of any ticketing platform. So NOFOMO would be one of the operators I would want as a customer eventually, not a competitor. If anything, this kind of layer makes ticketing platforms stickier.

### The ask (1 minute)

> Two things I want to check with you.
>
> One: anything you see from your seat that I might be missing? Concerns about how this lands with leadership, with PIF, with the team.
>
> Two: if at any point this becomes serious enough to need a different conversation — a pilot with NOFOMO eventually, a different arrangement with MDLBEAST, anything material — I want to do that transparently and early. Not surprise anyone.

### Then: listen

Pause. Do not fill the silence. The manager will respond. Match their energy and depth.

### Likely responses and how to handle each

| Response | Reply |
|---|---|
| Supportive: "thanks for telling me, sounds interesting, keep me posted." | "Will do. I will send a quick Slack summary later so we have it documented." |
| Concerned about time: "how are you balancing this with the day job?" | "Day job comes first, always has. Most of this is weekends. The validation phase is deliberately low-commitment so my day work does not suffer. If you ever feel it is, tell me directly." |
| Concerned about conflict with NOFOMO: "how is this not competition?" | "Fair question. Ticketing platforms have always had a support layer sitting on top — Zendesk, Intercom, etc. I am building the AI-native version of that, vertical to events. The way Shopify merchants use Gorgias for support, NOFOMO operators could use this. And strategically, I want NOFOMO as a customer, not a competitor." |
| Concerned about IP/data: "make sure you are not using anything MDLBEAST." | "Agreed and already doing that. Happy to have someone review the artifacts if it would make anyone more comfortable." |
| Concerned about optics: "I do not know how [bigger boss] would react." | "Understood. Two options: keep it quiet at validation stage, which is where it is, and revisit when there is something real. Or you can raise it to them now with the framing we have talked about. I lean toward the first because there is nothing to react to yet, but I want you comfortable with how this is held." |
| Pushback: "I am not comfortable with this." | "Okay. What specifically concerns you?" Then listen. Do not negotiate. Do not over-explain. If they say stop, you respect it and we re-strategize. If they say "let me think," give them time and follow up in writing in a few days. |
| Curious: "tell me more about the product, can I see the demo?" | "Sure, want to do it now or in a separate session?" This is a good outcome. Means engaged, not threatened. |
| Joking it off: "haha, going to fund my next startup eh?" | Light tone back, then redirect: "We will see. Right now I am just trying to figure out if there is something here. Wanted you to know either way." |

### Close (30 seconds)

> Appreciate you hearing me out. Going to send a quick Slack note summarizing what we talked about, just so we have it in writing. Nothing formal — alignment doc.

Do not ask permission to send the Slack note. Just tell them you are sending it.

## Section 3 — Written follow-up (Slack DM, within 4 hours)

This is the document that matters. It does three things: confirms what was discussed, creates a written record, and gives the manager one last chance to correct any misrepresentation. Send it within 4 hours so it is still fresh and they can adjust language while it is still in their head.

### Template A — If the conversation went well

> Thanks for the chat earlier. Quick recap so we are aligned:
>
> — I shared that I am exploring an AI customer support product for GCC live event operators. Validation phase only, simulator demo, no real customers, no company.
>
> — I confirmed: no MDLBEAST data, code, customer info, or work hours are involved. Demo content is synthetic.
>
> — I confirmed: NOFOMO is positioned as a potential future customer of this product, not a competitor.
>
> — You said [paraphrase what they said — supportive, cautious, whatever it was]. Appreciate that.
>
> — I will keep you in the loop. If anything material changes (going to market, taking outside capital, or anything that would change my arrangement with MDLBEAST), I will come to you first before it becomes public or formal.
>
> Let me know if I have misrepresented anything above. Otherwise, see you [next interaction].

### Template B — If the manager was cautious or asked for specific guardrails

> Thanks for the chat earlier. Quick recap so we are aligned:
>
> — I shared that I am exploring an AI customer support product for GCC live event operators. Validation phase only.
>
> — I confirmed: no MDLBEAST data, code, customer info, or work hours involved.
>
> — On your point about [whatever they raised — leadership awareness, IP review, conflict definition]: [specific commitment, e.g. "I will hold off on any operator outreach in the Saudi market until we have agreed how to handle it" or "I will keep this quiet at validation stage and re-raise with you before any external positioning"].
>
> — I will keep you in the loop on material changes, and re-raise this with you before anything moves from validation to a real venture.
>
> Let me know if this captures it accurately.

### Template C — If the manager wanted time to think

> Thanks for the chat earlier. I know you wanted to think on it — no urgency from my side. Quick recap of what we discussed so it does not lose shape between now and our next conversation:
>
> — I am exploring an AI customer support product for GCC live event operators. Validation phase only, simulator demo, no real activity.
>
> — Built on synthetic data, no MDLBEAST anything involved.
>
> — Framed as a potential NOFOMO customer relationship long-term, not competition.
>
> — You wanted time to think about [their specific concern], which is fair. Let me know when you have had a chance to sit with it. I am not moving on operator outreach or anything external until we have closed the loop.
>
> Happy to come back with more detail, the demo, or anything else that helps.

## What NOT to do

  1. Do not ask for permission. The conversation is informative, not a request. If you ask permission, you give the manager a yes/no problem they did not need to have.
  2. Do not over-explain or oversell. If you talk too much, you signal anxiety. Confidence is brief.
  3. Do not bring up: legal advice you have taken, fundraising plans, hires you are making, the contractor you are about to hire, the spec document, or anything that signals this is more advanced than "exploring." All of those become the conversation if you mention them, and none of them help.
  4. Do not promise things that depend on future circumstances. "I will never compete with NOFOMO" is the wrong commitment. "I am not building NOFOMO competition" is correct: it describes the present, not the future.
  5. Do not bring up the demo unless they ask. Showing it can be powerful but it changes the conversation from "let me tell you" to "let me sell you." Wrong frame.
  6. Do not negotiate against pushback in the moment. If the manager raises a concern, ask one clarifying question, then sit with it. Better to come back in writing than to commit on the spot.
  7. Do not skip the follow-up DM. The conversation is air. The DM is the record. If you do not write it down, it did not happen, from a legal or HR standpoint.
  8. Do not BCC anyone on the follow-up. Direct to manager only. If they want to loop someone else in, that is their call.

## Edge case: what if the manager says "stop"

It is a real possibility. Two scenarios:

**They say stop directly.** Respect it in the moment. Do not negotiate. Send a follow-up DM that documents what was said and what you will do. Then take 48 hours to reassess. Options at that point:

  - Pause the venture and stay at MDLBEAST. Real option if you decide the relationship is worth more than the venture.
  - Push back with new information. If their concern was based on a misunderstanding (e.g., they thought you were using MDLBEAST data when you are not), re-raise with the correction.
  - Decide the venture is the priority, plan an exit, leave on good terms. Your earlier message acknowledged this as a worst-case option. It is not the worst case in the absolute — it is the worst case for the manager-relationship goal of this conversation. But it preserves the venture.

**They say "I need to escalate."** This is harder. They have decided this is bigger than their decision to make. Options:

  - Let them escalate. You cannot stop it. Stay calm, send the follow-up DM documenting the conversation, do not preempt the escalation by going to their boss yourself. That makes things worse.
  - Pause all external activity (no operator outreach, no contractor hiring) until you hear back. The cost of waiting one week for a higher-level read is much lower than the cost of being mid-outreach when the decision comes down.

In both cases, the follow-up DM is critical. It establishes the timeline, your intent, and your commitments in writing. If anything escalates further (HR, legal), that DM is your record of having handled this transparently.
